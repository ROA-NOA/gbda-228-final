ABOUT.

The "Client Brand Design Assignment" partnered me with another student to bring their story to life. My partner was a self starter housing a myriad of interests from sculpting to digital design. This gave me a wide berth to explore ideas, leading to the creation of my favorite designs to date.

The project consisted of 4 sections.

1 - Client Interview

To begin, we had meetings with our partners to learn more about them.

Going into this I had two goals.
1 - Genuinely learn about my partner’s personality.
2 - Find something about their story to tie to their design.

Thanks to setting these goals before the interviews, organizing the information was much easier.

Notable parts of the interview included my partner’s self taught skills, and a certain sculpture they created of people walking toward a hole in the palm of a sculpted hand.

When asked about it, they told me it represented how we all age yet walk toward the same end.
A little dark, but inspiring nonetheless

2 - Logos

Following our discussions, the real work started. I learned of my partner's love for all things handicraft and decided to run with it. There are too many iterations to show them all, but here a few of the biggest checkpoints.

Phase 1 - Chosing the Shape

This phase had me playing with angles, rotation, backgrounds and the number of fragment in each design design.

It’s important to note that i had not decided on the line
“Out of Many, One”
at this stage. I was simply throwing ideas at the canvas until I found my bearings.

Phase 2 - ONE

In a stroke of inspiration I remembered the Latin phrase “E pluribus, enum,” or in English, “Out of many, one.”

This phrase was fitting considering my partner taught herself how to sculpt, sew and and paint with litte more than Youtube and a dream.

I also made a number of improvements in the hand, making th hole in the palm more ovular, the thumb take a sharper angle, and other small changes.

Phase 3 - Kaleidoscope

This was by far the most painful part of the assignment.

I switched through countless different colour palettes and saturation levels as I inched closer to what felt right. Definetly an uphill battle on this one.

Eventually, I had to take a break.

Staring at the project for hours on end was not helping progress. I felt like I was running in circles. I knew what vibe I wanted, but I couldn’t hit quite right....

That is until my friend started playing Alto’s Odessey on a discord call

The real problem wasn't the palette, but rather, where the focus was.

With the adjustments, the fingertips draw the eyes upward, rather than to the base as with my other designs

3 - Mockups

Not much to be said aside from that this quick exercise reinforced that small changes can have large effects when used properly. For example, the noise texture on the poster in the next part.

These mockups were simple, yet vital for testing our competency with Adobe Photoshop, a quick exercise in getting our points across.

4 - Out of Many, ONE

In the final section of the project, we took on the challenge of creating a poster for the brand using our logos and feedback from the “client”.

This was the biggest challenge of the project.

Getting the hand to look right on the background while avoiding a flat design caused me trouble for hours.

Only through the advice of my teachers and feedback of my peers was I settled on the gold inlay and drip from the ring

I believe this collaboration and iteration only makes the tagline of the project sweeter.

“OUT OF MANY, ONE”
