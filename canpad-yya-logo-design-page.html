Using Figma, I created a design for the Canpad Doctor Confference's Youth and Young adult branch. Resulting in a logo the team can use for social media, emails and merch. Bellow details the process I went through to get the end result.

0 - Background

As with most design projects, I started with some research.

CANPAD or the Cannadian Association of Nigerian Physicians and Dentists is a non-profit organization with the mission of improving the health sector in both Canada and Nigeria through charity programs, mentorship, networking, scholarships, and most importantly, their Annual Doctors Conference in the shape of a family event.

With the large family presence af the conference, the young attendees have grown each year alongside the conference. Recently, the organization decided to put emphasis on this group, assigning them a comittee.
That's where I come in.

1 - Moodboard and Sketches

Background established, the Y&YA team wanted a logo that incorporated elements of the organization's.
Elements like colour pallette, flags, and overal shape were on the list items to include.

Additionally, they sent pictures of one of their merch t-shirts, saying they want to go in the same direction if possible.
I'll be honest, I ate the Y2K bait.

Below are the moodboard and sketches.

Firstly, the moodboard. Taking into account the whispers of a Y2K asesthetic, I collected some typeface designs online and paired them with the shirt photo, CANPAD's official Logo and a colour pallete.

For the sketches, I started by experiementing with how the letters coult fit together, but eventually decided that simplicity was best.

While sketching I tried playing with the angle of the letters, occationally mixing in the Staff of Aesculapius, a common symbol of the medical field. Some designs also feature the Canadian and Nigerian flags.

2 - The Designs

Once the sketches were completed, I sent the photo to the representatitve of the Y&Ya team who brought it to their meeting that week. Before long, they got back to me with their favorites and I began work putting together the designs in Figma.

We chose to work on 4 to make full logos for, and of them they would choose the best one.

The process was highly itterative with frequent communication between the myself and the Y&YA team. They were very vocal about the needed adjustments, and I was more than happy to oblige.

Everything from the need to include flags to the word "CANPAD" in each design was fine tuned to reach the end result.

Once adjustments were done, the Youth and Young adults Team landed on this logo to proceed with...
Until the organization executives decided otherwise.

3 - Final Product

In the end, we landed on this design instead.

Funnily enough, this one was my favorite of the designs I created here. I won't lie, I'm glad on that end.

This project taught me alot about managing external opinions, and not to get too attached to my designs. Rapid itteration is key.

This project was a solid reminder of the need to make things exist first, and making them good at the soonest oppertunity.
